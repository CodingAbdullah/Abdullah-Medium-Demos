# HyperLiquid — A Dummy's Guide

HyperLiquid is a high-performance Layer-1 blockchain built specifically for decentralized finance, featuring a fully on-chain orderbook perpetuals exchange with sub-second finality.

---

## What is HyperLiquid?

HyperLiquid is a decentralized exchange (DEX) that operates entirely on-chain using its own purpose-built Layer-1 blockchain — HyperEVM. Unlike most DeFi protocols that rely on Ethereum or other general-purpose chains, HyperLiquid was engineered from the ground up to support high-frequency trading with the performance characteristics of a centralized exchange.

Key characteristics:

- **On-chain Orderbook** — All orders, matches, and settlements happen transparently on-chain
- **Sub-second Finality** — Block times of ~100ms enable near-instant trade execution
- **Permissionless Listings** — Anyone can list a spot asset via the HIP-1 standard
- **No Gas Fees on Perps** — Perpetual trades are gas-free for users
- **Self-custodial** — Users retain full control of their assets at all times

---

## Core Components

- **Perpetuals (Perps)**
  Leveraged derivative contracts that allow traders to go long or short on assets without expiry. HyperLiquid supports hundreds of perp markets with cross-margin and isolated-margin modes.

- **Spot Trading**
  Native spot markets where users can trade real assets directly on-chain. New tokens can be deployed and listed permissionlessly using the HIP-1 standard.

- **HyperEVM**
  An EVM-compatible execution environment running on top of the HyperLiquid L1, enabling smart contract deployments that interact directly with the native orderbook.

- **Vaults**
  Liquidity pools that allow users to deposit funds and earn a share of trading profits generated by vault strategies. Users can also create their own vaults.

- **HYPE Token**
  The native token of the HyperLiquid ecosystem, used for staking, governance, and fee discounts across the platform.

## HyperLiquid Improvement Proposals (HIPs)

HIPs govern the evolution of the HyperLiquid protocol. Key proposals include:

- **HIP-1** — Standard for permissionless spot token deployment and listing on the native orderbook
- **HIP-2** — Defines perpetual liquidity provisioning standards for newly listed spot assets

## Trading Concepts

**Leverage**
Perps allow up to high multiples of leverage. Leverage amplifies both gains and losses — position sizing and risk management are essential.

**Funding Rates**
Periodic payments exchanged between long and short positions to keep perpetual prices anchored to the underlying spot price. A positive funding rate means longs pay shorts; negative means shorts pay longs.

**Liquidation**
When a position's margin falls below the maintenance margin requirement, it is liquidated automatically. Always monitor your margin ratio.

**Cross vs. Isolated Margin**
- *Cross-margin*: Shares the full account balance as collateral across all positions
- *Isolated-margin*: Allocates a fixed amount of collateral to a single position, limiting max loss to that amount

---

## Key Safety Rules

- Never go "all-in" — allocating your entire account to a single trade is akin to degenerate gambling and exposes you to total loss
- Always use stop-losses to cap downside on leveraged positions
- Monitor funding rates — holding a position while funding works against you erodes returns silently
- Start with low leverage until you understand how margin, liquidation, and funding interact
- Only trade with capital you can afford to lose
